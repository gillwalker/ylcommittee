Young Life Forward: A Movement Has Begun

Young Life’s renewed vision for thriving, sustainable ministry — four mission strategies to double worldwide impact.

← Back to Onboarding Home

A Movement Has Begun

Young Life is in the lives of more than two million kids in over 100 countries worldwide. As we have prayed, discussed and planned, we believe a renewed vision is coming into focus — a plan for thriving, sustainable ministry called Forward, because that’s our gospel reality in Young Life: we lean into things, our hearts beat for the new and the next.

Philippians 3:13–14 (NASB) “… I do not regard myself as having laid hold of it yet; but one thing I do: forgetting what lies behind and reaching forward to what lies ahead, I press on toward the goal for the prize of the upward call of God in Christ Jesus.”

Four Mission Strategies

Deeper in Christ

Seeking the Lord First and Helping Kids Grow in Their Faith
  • Discipleship and Campaigners Initiative
  • Staff Enrichment Initiative

Ask every staff and leader to create a spiritual formation plan toward Christ-likeness, and ask every area to develop a discipleship plan that doubles the number of kids involved in Campaigners.

Together

Building and Equipping Teams that Reflect the Worldwide Body of Christ
  • Leadership Diversity Initiative
  • Leadership Development and Training
  • Key Volunteers Focus

Increase the diversity (gender, race, country of origin, abilities) of staff by 50 percent, with a focus at each level to reach more kids and ultimately reflect the cultures in which we minister.

Innovation

Creating New Ways Today to Reach Kids Tomorrow
  • Ideas Incubator
  • Unique Kid Groups
  • Innovation in Camping
  • Enhanced Infrastructure

Encourage an innovative culture around our methods to create, translate, and replicate successful ways of reaching kids.

Growth

Impacting All Kids — Within Sight and Just Out of Reach
  • Major Cities Initiative
  • Growth in Every Life Stage Category and Focus Ministry
  • Camping Expansion
  • Existing Club Growth

Build relationships with more kids so average weekly club attendance grows from 300,000 to 600,000.
